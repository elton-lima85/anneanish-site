About me

Anne Anish is a dedicated community leader, reformist, and passionate advocate for people-centric development. With years of active involvement in local initiatives, she has consistently stood for transparency, accountability, and inclusivity in governance. Having survived a life-threatening health challenge, Anne considers this her second life — one she has chosen to dedicate fully in service of people. Guided by her deep faith and sense of purpose, she embraces the philosophy of servant leadership, where true leadership means listening, caring, and working alongside the community. Education is at the heart of her vision. Anne firmly believes that every child deserves access to quality education, and she tirelessly works so that no child is left behind. For her, empowering children through learning is the strongest foundation for a better tomorrow. Beyond education, Anne actively supports good causes and reform initiatives that strengthen society. Whether it is championing environmental protection, promoting women’s empowerment, or standing for social justice, she believes in progressive change that benefits all. As she steps forward for the upcoming corporation election, Anne envisions a city where sustainable growth, quality education, better civic amenities, and stronger community participation go hand in hand. Her commitment is not just to lead but to listen, to build bridges, and to work tirelessly for the welfare of all. Anne Anish – A Voice for the People, A Vision for the Future.

Anne believes in transparent, accountable governance with regular ward meetings and public dashboards.

Social activities undertaken

14 July 2025 — AAP’s Follow-up Gets New Teacher for Bopodi School Bopodi, Pune:

Bopodi, Pune: Due to Aam Aadmi Party’s consistent follow-up, Patasibai Chhajed E-Learning English Medium School in Bopodi has finally received a new teacher after facing shortages since last year. Last year, 40 nursery students were promoted to Class 1, but lack of teachers forced 25 parents to withdraw admissions. Parents approached AAP, which raised the issue with the Pune Municipal Corporation. With a new teacher now appointed, several parents are planning to re-enroll their children. AAP leaders Annie Anish, Sheetal Kandelkar, Vikas Chavan, and Shankar Thorat, along with parents, had met PMC officials to press for action. AAP has further demanded that the PMC urgently provide adequate teachers in civic schools to ensure quality education for children from underprivileged families.
